Refund Policy

Effective date: 1 January 2025. Last updated: 1 January 2025.

We want you to be happy with your tailored resume. Please read this policy carefully so you understand when refunds apply.

Australian Consumer Law: Nothing in this refund policy limits or excludes your rights under the Australian Consumer Law (ACL), including your right to a remedy for a failure that is a major failure or is not of acceptable quality.

1. Nature of the Service

CVEZ provides a digital document service. Once your tailored resume has been generated and made available for download, the service has been delivered in full. Because the output is a digital document that is immediately accessible after payment, we are unable to offer refunds based solely on a change of mind.

2. When You Are Entitled to a Refund

You are entitled to a full refund in the following circumstances:

  • The service failed to generate a tailored resume due to a technical error on our end
  • Your resume content was not used in the output and the result bears no relevance to your uploaded document
  • You were charged but the download was not made available to you
  • There was a duplicate or accidental charge on your account

3. When Refunds Do Not Apply

Refunds will generally not be issued in the following situations:

  • You are dissatisfied with the quality or style of the rewritten resume but it was successfully generated and delivered
  • You provided an incorrect or incomplete resume file
  • You provided a job listing that was unclear or incomplete
  • You changed your mind after the resume was generated and made available
  • You found alternative employment or decided not to apply for the role

4. How to Request a Refund

To request a refund, please use the Contact Us form within 7 days of your purchase, selecting "Refund request" as the subject and including your order details and reason for the request. We will review your request and respond within 3 business days.

Approved refunds will be returned to the original payment method within 5 to 10 business days, depending on your bank or card provider.

5. Disputes

If you are not satisfied with the outcome of your refund request, you may contact your card provider to raise a dispute, or seek assistance from the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) at accc.gov.au.
